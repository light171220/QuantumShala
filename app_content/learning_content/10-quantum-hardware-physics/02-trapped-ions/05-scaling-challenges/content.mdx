## 1. The Scaling Challenge

Trapped ions offer exceptional qubit quality, but scaling brings unique challenges.

## 2. Mode Crowding

As ions are added, motional mode frequencies bunch together:
- Hard to address individual modes
- Cross-talk between modes during gates
- Heating affects more modes

Practical limit: ~20-50 ions in a single chain

## 3. Addressing Difficulties

Individual addressing requires:
- Tight laser focusing (~1 micron)
- Ion spacing decreases with more ions
- Crosstalk to neighboring ions

## 4. Scaling Approaches

### Photonic Interconnects
- Entangle ions in separate traps via photons
- Demonstrated between distant ions
- Challenge: Low success probability (~10^-6 per attempt)

### QCCD Architecture
- Shuttle ions between zones
- Small interaction zones, large storage zones
- Being pursued by Quantinuum, others

### 2D Arrays
- Multiple linear chains in 2D arrangement
- Coupling via shared modes or shuttling

## 5. Current State of the Art

| Company | Approach | System Size |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| IonQ | Long chains | 32+ qubits |

## Summary

Scaling trapped ions requires new architectures beyond single chains. The QCCD approach and photonic interconnects are leading candidates.
