## Learning Objectives

- Derive the normal modes of a linear ion chain
- Calculate mode frequencies and participation vectors
- Understand the role of motional modes in entangling gates
- Analyze heating rates and their impact on gate fidelity

## 1. Collective Motion in Ion Chains

When multiple ions are trapped together, their individual motions couple through Coulomb interaction, creating **collective normal modes**.

## 2. Normal Mode Analysis

### Equations of Motion

For N ions at equilibrium positions z_i^0, small displacements satisfy:

<Formula>
$$m\ddot{u}_i = -m\omega_z^2 u_i + \sum_{j\neq i} K_{ij}(u_j - u_i)$$
</Formula>

where K_ij is the Coulomb spring constant.

### Mode Frequencies

For two ions: COM mode at omega_z, stretch mode at sqrt(3)*omega_z.

For N ions: N axial modes with frequencies omega_z < omega_1 < omega_2 < ... < omega_{N-1}.

## 3. The Quantum Data Bus

Motional modes enable entangling gates:
1. Laser couples ion internal state to motion
2. Motion mediates interaction between distant ions
3. After gate, motion returns to initial state (disentangled)

### Lamb-Dicke Parameter

The coupling strength is characterized by:

<Formula>
$$\eta = k \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2m\omega}}$$
</Formula>

where k is the laser wave vector. Typical values: eta ~ 0.05-0.2.

## 4. Heating and Decoherence

### Anomalous Heating

Electric field noise heats the ion motion:

<Formula>
$$\dot{\bar{n}} = \frac{S_E(\omega)}{4m\hbar\omega}$$
</Formula>

Heating rates of 1-100 phonons/ms are typical, depending on trap technology.

## Summary

Motional modes are essential for trapped ion quantum computing:
1. Collective modes arise from Coulomb coupling
2. Modes serve as quantum bus for entangling gates
3. Lamb-Dicke parameter governs spin-motion coupling
4. Anomalous heating limits gate fidelity
