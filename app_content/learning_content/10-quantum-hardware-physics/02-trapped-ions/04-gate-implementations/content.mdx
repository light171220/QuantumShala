## Learning Objectives

- Understand single-qubit gates using stimulated Raman transitions
- Explain the Cirac-Zoller gate mechanism
- Describe the Molmer-Sorensen gate and its advantages
- Calculate gate fidelities and error sources

## 1. Single-Qubit Gates

### Rabi Oscillations

A resonant laser drives coherent oscillations between qubit states:

<Formula>
$$|\psi(t)\rangle = \cos(\Omega t/2)|0\rangle - i\sin(\Omega t/2)|1\rangle$$
</Formula>

Gate times: ~1-10 microseconds for pi pulses.

### Arbitrary Rotations

- **X gates**: Laser on resonance with controlled phase
- **Z gates**: AC Stark shifts or frequency detuning
- **Arbitrary**: Combine X and Z rotations

## 2. The Cirac-Zoller Gate

The original proposal (1995) for trapped ion entanglement:

1. Map qubit 1 state to motional mode
2. Conditional operation on qubit 2 based on motion
3. Unmap motion back to qubit 1

Requires ground-state cooling and is sequential.

## 3. Molmer-Sorensen Gate

### The Key Innovation

Apply bichromatic light (two frequencies) near the red and blue sidebands simultaneously:

<Formula>
$$\omega_1 = \omega_0 + \omega_m - \delta, \quad \omega_2 = \omega_0 - \omega_m + \delta$$
</Formula>

### The Interaction

Creates a state-dependent force that:
- Displaces motional states in circles in phase space
- Different internal states acquire different geometric phases
- Motion returns to origin, but phase remains

### Result

Generates the entangling operation:

<Formula>
$$U_{MS} = \exp\left(-i\frac{\pi}{4}\sigma_x^{(1)}\sigma_x^{(2)}\right)$$
</Formula>

### Advantages

- Works at finite temperature (doesn't require n=0)
- Parallel on all ions
- High fidelity achieved (>99.9%)

## 4. Error Sources

### Major Error Mechanisms

1. **Motional decoherence**: Heating during gate
2. **Laser intensity noise**: Rabi frequency fluctuations
3. **Laser phase noise**: Especially for hyperfine qubits
4. **Off-resonant coupling**: To spectator modes
5. **Spontaneous emission**: From intermediate states

### State of the Art

- Single-qubit: >99.99% fidelity
- Two-qubit: >99.9% fidelity (best: 99.95%)

## Summary

Trapped ion gates achieve exceptional fidelities:
1. Single-qubit gates via resonant laser pulses
2. Molmer-Sorensen gate is the workhorse for entanglement
3. Geometric phase mechanism provides robustness
4. Error sources are well-understood and controlled
