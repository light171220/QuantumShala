## 1. The Roadmap to Quantum Utility

The quantum computing industry has transitioned from pure research to commercial development, with vendors publishing detailed roadmaps toward practical quantum computers.

<Definition term="Quantum Utility">
The stage where quantum computers provide measurable value for real-world problems, even without full error correction. IBM declared "quantum utility" achieved in 2023 with their 127-qubit Eagle processor demonstrating classically-intractable computations.
</Definition>

### Key Milestones Framework

| Milestone | Definition | Status (2024) |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| NISQ Era | Noisy intermediate-scale quantum | Current |
| Quantum Utility | Useful noisy calculations | Emerging |
| Early Fault Tolerance | Limited logical qubits | 2025-2027 |
| Full Fault Tolerance | Large-scale error-corrected | 2030+ |
| Quantum Advantage | Practical superiority | Application-dependent |

## 3. Google Quantum AI Roadmap

### Path to Error-Corrected Quantum Computing

Google focuses on demonstrating scientific milestones toward fault tolerance.

**Key Milestones:**

| Year | Achievement | Significance |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 2019 | Quantum supremacy | First beyond-classical computation |
| 2023 | Below threshold | Surface code errors decrease with size |
| 2025 | Logical qubit | Useful error-corrected qubit |
| 2027-2029 | Logical processor | Multiple logical qubits |
| 2030+ | 1M physical qubits | Large-scale fault tolerance |

### Error Correction Progress

<Formula>
$$\epsilon_{\text{logical}} = c \times \epsilon_{\text{physical}}^{\lceil d/2 \rceil}$$
</Formula>

Google's 2023 demonstration showed logical error rates decreasing as code distance increased - the first time below the error correction threshold.

### Willow Processor Achievements

```python
# Google's error correction scaling demonstration
google_ec_data = {
    'distance_3': {'logical_error': 3.0e-2},

    'distance_7': {'logical_error': 3.0e-3},  # Extrapolated
}

# Shows exponential suppression: error ~ (p/p_th)^(d/2)
```

| Year | System | Qubits | #AQ | Key Feature |

| 2023 | Forte | 32 | 29 | Native XX gates |
| 2024 | Forte Enterprise | 35 | 32 | Reconfigurable zones |
| 2025 | - | 64+ | 64+ | Multi-core |
| 2028 | - | 1024+ | - | Photonic interconnects |

### Modular Architecture

IonQ's path to scale relies on **photonic interconnects** between multiple ion trap modules.

<Definition term="Networked Ion Traps">
Multiple ion trap processors connected via photon-mediated entanglement, enabling distributed quantum computing with all-to-all connectivity within modules and high-fidelity links between modules.
</Definition>

## 7. Error Correction Timeline

### Industry Consensus View

<Warning>
Most experts agree that useful fault-tolerant quantum computing is at least 5-10 years away (2030+). Near-term progress will come from:

- More efficient error correction codes
- Application-specific error mitigation
</Warning>

## 8. Technology Disruption Possibilities

### Potential Game-Changers

| Technology | Potential Impact | Timeline |
|------------|------------------|----------|
| Topological qubits | Built-in error protection | Unknown |
| Room-temperature qubits | Eliminates cryogenics | Long-term |
| Photonic scaling | Deterministic photon-photon | 5-10 years |
| Cryo-CMOS | Eliminates wiring bottleneck | 3-5 years |
| New QEC codes | Reduce overhead dramatically | Ongoing |

### Microsoft's Topological Approach

Microsoft pursues a fundamentally different path using **topological qubits**:

<Formula>
$$\text{Error rate} \propto e^{-\Delta/k_BT}$$
</Formula>

where $\Delta$ is the topological gap - potentially providing inherent protection against local errors.

### The "Quantum Advantage" Question

<Example>
**Realistic Assessment:**

True quantum advantage requires:
1. **Correctness**: Results must be verifiable
2. **Utility**: Must solve a useful problem
3. **Superiority**: Must beat best classical approaches
4. **Practicality**: Must be economically viable

Currently, no application satisfies all four criteria for commercial use.
</Example>

## 11. What to Watch For

### Near-term Indicators (2024-2026)

1. **Logical qubit demonstrations** that outperform physical qubits
2. **Error correction scaling** - does adding more qubits help?
3. **Application benchmarks** - practical problem performance
4. **Cost reduction** - making quantum accessible

### Medium-term Milestones (2026-2030)

1. **100+ logical qubits** operating together
2. **Quantum advantage** in at least one practical application
3. **Error rates** consistently below 10^-6 for logical operations
4. **Hybrid workflows** seamlessly mixing classical and quantum

## 12. Summary

Key takeaways on quantum computing roadmaps:

1. **All major vendors** have published aggressive roadmaps
2. **Error correction** is the critical path to utility
3. **Fault tolerance** is realistically 5-10+ years away
4. **Near-term value** may come from hybrid approaches
5. **Application-specific** timelines vary significantly
6. **Disruptions** (topological, new codes) could accelerate progress

<Tip>
Be skeptical of claims about quantum computing timelines. The technology is genuinely advancing, but many announcements are optimistic. Focus on demonstrated results rather than projected capabilities when making decisions.
</Tip>
