## 1. Introduction to Catalysis

<Definition term="Catalysis">
The acceleration of a chemical reaction by a substance (catalyst) that participates in the reaction but is regenerated and not consumed in the overall process.
</Definition>

```python

    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nCatalyst Types:")
    print("-" * 45)
    types = [
        ("Homogeneous", "Same phase as reactants", "Organometallics"),
        ("Heterogeneous", "Different phase (usually solid)", "Metal surfaces"),
        ("Enzymatic", "Biological catalysts", "Proteins"),
        ("Photocatalysis", "Light-driven", "TiO₂, semiconductors"),
        ("Electrocatalysis", "Electrochemical", "Fuel cells"),
    ]
    print(f"  {'Type':<15} {'Description':<30} {'Example'}")

    print("  2. Activation: Bond breaking/forming")
    print("  3. Reaction: Chemical transformation")
```

## 2. Reaction Energetics

```python
def reaction_energetics():
    """Reaction energetics from DFT."""

    print("Reaction Energetics from DFT")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nPotential Energy Surface:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  A + B → [A---B]‡ → C + D")
    print()
    print("  Key points:")
    print("    • Reactants (initial state)")
    print("    • Transition state (TS, saddle point)")
    print("    • Intermediates (local minima)")
    print("    • Products (final state)")

    print("\nEnergy Quantities:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Reaction energy: ΔE = E_products - E_reactants")
    print("  Activation energy: Ea = E_TS - E_reactants")
    print("  Desorption energy: E_des = E_gas + E_surface - E_adsorbed")
    print()
    print("  Thermodynamic: ΔE determines feasibility")
    print("  Kinetic: Ea determines rate")

    print("\nTransition State Theory:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  k = (kT/h) × exp(-Ea/RT) × exp(ΔS‡/R)")
    print()
    print("  Pre-exponential factor: A ≈ kT/h ≈ 10¹³ s⁻¹")
    print()
    print("  At 500 K:")
    for Ea in [0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0]:
        k = 1e13 * np.exp(-Ea * 96485 / (8.314 * 500))
        print(f"    Ea = {Ea} eV → k ≈ {k:.2e} s⁻¹")

    print("\nFinding Transition States:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Methods:")
    print("    • NEB (Nudged Elastic Band)")
    print("    • CI-NEB (Climbing Image)")
    print("    • Dimer method")
    print("    • QST2/QST3 (Gaussian)")

reaction_energetics()
```

<Formula name="Arrhenius Rate Equation">
$$k = A \exp\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_BT}\right)$$

Where $A$ is the pre-exponential factor (~10¹³ s⁻¹ for surface reactions), $E_a$ is the activation energy, $k_B$ is Boltzmann's constant, and $T$ is temperature.
</Formula>

## 4. Adsorption Energies

```python
def adsorption_energies():
    """Calculating adsorption energies from DFT."""

    print("Adsorption Energies from DFT")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nDefinition:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  E_ads = E_surface+adsorbate - E_surface - E_adsorbate")
    print()
    print("  Negative = exothermic (favorable)")
    print("  Positive = endothermic (unfavorable)")

    print("\nTypes of Adsorption:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Physisorption: Weak (van der Waals)")
    print("    E_ads ≈ -0.1 to -0.5 eV")
    print("    No significant charge transfer")
    print()
    print("  Chemisorption: Strong (chemical bond)")
    print("    E_ads ≈ -0.5 to -3 eV")
    print("    Significant charge transfer")

    print("\nAdsorption Sites (FCC 111):")
    print("-" * 45)
    sites = [
        ("Top", "On top of surface atom", "1 metal"),
        ("Bridge", "Between two atoms", "2 metals"),
        ("FCC hollow", "Above subsurface atom", "3 metals"),
        ("HCP hollow", "Above surface atom", "3 metals"),
    ]
    for site, description, coord in sites:
        print(f"  {site}: {description}")
        print(f"    Coordination: {coord}")

    print("\nExample: H on Pt(111)")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Top site: E_ads ≈ -2.6 eV")
    print("  FCC hollow: E_ads ≈ -2.7 eV (most stable)")
    print("  Bridge: E_ads ≈ -2.65 eV")
    print()
    print("  Typical DFT accuracy: ±0.1-0.2 eV")

adsorption_energies()
```

<Tip>
Always test multiple adsorption sites and configurations. The most stable site determines the coverage-dependent behavior and is crucial for kinetic modeling.
</Tip>
