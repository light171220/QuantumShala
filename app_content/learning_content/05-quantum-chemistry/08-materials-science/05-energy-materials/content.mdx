## 1. Battery Materials

<Definition term="Battery Materials Design">
Computational screening and optimization of electrode materials (anodes, cathodes) and electrolytes for rechargeable batteries, focusing on voltage, capacity, stability, and ion transport.
</Definition>


## 2. Ion Transport

```python
def ion_transport():
    """Ion transport in battery materials."""

    print("Ion Transport Calculations")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nDiffusion Mechanisms:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Vacancy mechanism: Ion hops to adjacent vacancy")
    print("  Interstitial: Ion moves through interstitial sites")
    print("  Concerted: Multiple ions move cooperatively")

    print("\nNudged Elastic Band (NEB):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Find minimum energy path for migration")
    print()
    print("  Steps:")
    print("    1. Define initial and final positions")
    print("    2. Interpolate intermediate images")
    print("    3. Optimize path with spring forces")
    print("    4. Find saddle point (barrier)")
    print()
    print("  Migration barrier E_a:")
    print("    D = D₀ exp(-E_a/kT)")
    print("    Typical: E_a = 0.2-0.5 eV for fast conductors")

    print("\nMD for Diffusion:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Ab initio MD at elevated temperature")
    print()
    print("  Extract D from MSD:")
    print("  <r²(t)> = 6Dt (3D)")
    print()
    print("  Arrhenius fit:")
    print("  D(T) = D₀ exp(-E_a/kT)")

    print("\nSolid Electrolytes:")
    print("-" * 45)
    electrolytes = [
        ("LLZO", "Li₇La₃Zr₂O₁₂", 0.3, 0.1-1),
        ("LGPS", "Li₁₀GeP₂S₁₂", 0.25, 12),
        ("LPS", "Li₃PS₄", 0.35, 0.1),
        ("NASICON", "Li₁.₃Al₀.₃Ti₁.₇(PO₄)₃", 0.3, 1),
    ]
    print(f"  {'Abbrev':<10} {'Formula':<22} {'E_a(eV)':<10} {'σ(mS/cm)'}")
    print("  " + "-" * 55)
    for abbr, formula, ea, sigma in electrolytes:
        print(f"  {abbr:<10} {formula:<22} {ea:<10.2f} {sigma}")

ion_transport()
```

<Formula name="Migration Barrier and Diffusivity">
$$D = D_0 \exp\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_B T}\right) \quad \text{and} \quad \sigma = \frac{nq^2D}{k_BT}$$

Where $E_a$ is the migration barrier, $D_0$ is the pre-factor, and $\sigma$ is the ionic conductivity.
</Formula>

```python
        ("Pd", 0.6, -20, "Fast, expensive"),

```

<Tip>
For hydrogen storage, the thermodynamics (ΔH) must be balanced. DFT can screen materials, but kinetics often limit practical performance. Always consider activation barriers for H₂ dissociation.
</Tip>
