## Learning Objectives

- Apply entropy to quantify entanglement in quantum systems
- Use entropy in quantum thermodynamics and heat engines
- Analyze entropy in quantum error correction
- Study entropy scaling in many-body systems
- Connect entropy to quantum computational resources

## 1. Entanglement Quantification

### Entanglement Entropy

For pure bipartite states:
$$E(|\psi\rangle_{AB}) = S(\rho_A) = S(\rho_B)$$

This is the unique entanglement measure for pure states.

### Entanglement of Formation

For mixed states, the entanglement of formation:
$$E_F(\rho) = \min_{\{p_i, |\psi_i\rangle\}} \sum_i p_i E(|\psi_i\rangle)$$

## 2. Quantum Thermodynamics

### Second Law

The quantum second law: for a system coupled to a thermal bath,
$$\Delta S_{\text{total}} = \Delta S_{\text{system}} + \Delta S_{\text{bath}} \geq 0$$

### Landauer's Principle

Erasing one bit of information requires at least $k_B T \ln 2$ of energy.

### Quantum Heat Engines

Efficiency is bounded by Carnot:
$$\eta \leq 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$$

## 3. Many-Body Physics

### Area Law

For gapped ground states in d dimensions:
$$S(A) \propto |\partial A|$$

Entropy scales with boundary, not volume.

### Critical Systems

At criticality (1D CFT):
$$S(L) = \frac{c}{3} \log L + \text{const}$$

where c is the central charge.

## 4. Error Correction

### Coherent Information

The quantum capacity involves coherent information:
$$I_c(\rho, \mathcal{N}) = S(\mathcal{N}(\rho)) - S(E, \rho)$$

### Decoupling

Successful error correction requires decoupling environment from encoded information.

## Summary

Quantum entropy has wide-ranging applications:
- Entanglement quantification
- Thermodynamic bounds
- Many-body physics (area laws, criticality)
- Error correction (quantum capacity)

These applications demonstrate the fundamental role of entropy in quantum theory.
