## 1. Statement of Strong Subadditivity

<Definition term="Strong Subadditivity (SSA)">
For any tripartite quantum state $\rho_{ABC}$:

$$S(ABC) + S(B) \leq S(AB) + S(BC)$$
</Definition>

Equivalently:
$$S(A|BC) \leq S(A|B)$$

or
$$I(A:C|B) \geq 0$$

where $I(A:C|B) = S(A|B) - S(A|BC)$ is the conditional mutual information.

<Tip>
SSA says that conditioning on more systems reduces uncertainty. It's the quantum analogue of the classical chain rule property.
</Tip>

## 2. Equivalent Forms

### Form 1: Conditional Entropy Reduction
$$S(A|BC) \leq S(A|B)$$

### Form 2: Conditional Mutual Information Non-negativity
$$I(A:C|B) = S(AB) + S(BC) - S(ABC) - S(B) \geq 0$$

### Form 3: Monotonicity of Mutual Information
$$I(A:BC) \geq I(A:B)$$

## 3. Proof Sketch

SSA was proven by Lieb and Ruskai (1973). The proof uses:

1. **Lieb's concavity theorem**: The map $A \mapsto \text{Tr}(K^* A^p K B^{1-p})$ is concave for $0 \leq p \leq 1$.

2. **Joint convexity of relative entropy**: $D(\rho \| \sigma)$ is jointly convex.

3. **Monotonicity of relative entropy**: $D(\mathcal{E}(\rho) \| \mathcal{E}(\sigma)) \leq D(\rho \| \sigma)$.

## 4. Saturation Conditions

<Definition term="SSA Saturation">
Strong subadditivity saturates (equality holds) if and only if $\rho_{ABC}$ is a **quantum Markov chain**:

$$\rho_{ABC} = \rho_{AB}^{1/2} (\rho_B^{-1/2} \rho_{BC} \rho_B^{-1/2}) \rho_{AB}^{1/2}$$
</Definition>

This means there exists a recovery map $\mathcal{R}_B$ such that:
$$\rho_{ABC} = \mathcal{R}_B(\rho_{AB})$$

The system C can be recovered from B alone.

## 5. Consequences

### Araki-Lieb Inequality
From SSA with C trivial:
$$|S(A) - S(B)| \leq S(AB)$$

### Subadditivity
From SSA with B trivial:
$$S(AC) \leq S(A) + S(C)$$

### Monogamy of Entanglement
If A is maximally entangled with B, it cannot be entangled with C:
$$S(A|B) = -S(A) \implies S(A|BC) = -S(A) \implies I(A:C|B) = 0$$

## 6. Applications

### Holographic Entanglement Entropy
In AdS/CFT, SSA constrains the geometry of bulk spacetime:
- Ryu-Takayanagi formula relates entropy to minimal surfaces
- SSA becomes geometric: areas of minimal surfaces must satisfy SSA

### Quantum Many-Body Systems
- Area laws: Ground state entropy scales with boundary area
- Entanglement entropy in critical systems
- Topological entanglement entropy

### Error Correction
- Approximate quantum error correction
- Recovery maps for degraded quantum information

SSA encodes fundamental constraints on quantum correlations.
