## 1. Semiconductor Properties

<Definition term="Semiconductor">
A material with a band gap between 0.1 and 4 eV, where conductivity can be controlled by doping, temperature, or electric field, enabling electronic device applications.
</Definition>

## 2. Carrier Transport

```python
def carrier_transport():
    """Carrier transport properties from first principles."""

    print("Carrier Transport Properties")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nMobility:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  μ = eτ/m*")
    print()
    print("  τ = scattering time (from phonons, impurities, etc.)")
    print("  m* = effective mass")
    print()
    print("  High μ requires:")
    print("    • Small m* (light carriers)")
    print("    • Long τ (weak scattering)")

    print("\nScattering Mechanisms:")
    print("-" * 45)
    mechanisms = [
        ("Acoustic phonon", "~T^(-3/2)", "Deformation potential"),
        ("Optical phonon", "~exp(ℏω/kT)", "Polar materials"),
        ("Ionized impurity", "~T^(3/2)/N_i", "Doped materials"),
        ("Alloy scattering", "~x(1-x)", "Random alloys"),
    ]
    for mech, temp_dep, notes in mechanisms:
        print(f"  • {mech}")
        print(f"    T-dependence: {temp_dep}")
        print(f"    Notes: {notes}")

    print("\nDFT-Based Transport:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Steps:")
    print("    1. Calculate band structure E(k)")
    print("    2. Compute electron-phonon coupling g_qν")
    print("    3. Calculate scattering rates 1/τ")
    print("    4. Solve Boltzmann transport equation")
    print()
    print("  Codes: EPW, Perturbo, AMSET")

    print("\nSeebeck Coefficient:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  S = (kT/e) × (d ln σ / dE)|_{E_F}")
    print()
    print("  For thermoelectrics: Large S with high σ")
    print("  ZT = S²σT/κ (figure of merit)")

carrier_transport()
```

## 3. Doping and Defects

```python
def doping_defects():
    """Modeling doping and defects in semiconductors."""

    print("Doping and Defects")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nDefect Formation Energy:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  E_f[X^q] = E[X^q] - E[bulk] - Σnᵢμᵢ + q(E_VBM + E_F)")
    print()
    print("  Terms:")
    print("    E[X^q]: Total energy with defect")
    print("    E[bulk]: Perfect crystal energy")
    print("    nᵢμᵢ: Chemical potential contribution")
    print("    q(E_VBM + E_F): Charge state contribution")

    print("\nCharge Transition Levels:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ε(q/q') = [E_f(q) - E_f(q')]/(q'-q) - E_VBM")
    print()
    print("  Deep levels: Bad for devices (traps)")
    print("  Shallow levels: Good dopants")

    print("\nCommon Dopants:")
    print("-" * 45)
    dopants = [
        ("Si n-type", "P, As, Sb", "Group V → excess e⁻"),
        ("Si p-type", "B, Al, Ga", "Group III → excess h⁺"),
        ("GaN n-type", "Si on Ga", "Shallow donor"),
        ("GaN p-type", "Mg on Ga", "Deep, compensated"),
    ]
    for system, dopant, mechanism in dopants:
        print(f"  {system}:")
        print(f"    Dopants: {dopant}")
        print(f"    Mechanism: {mechanism}")

    print("\nSupercell Approach:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Use supercell to dilute defect")
    print("  Typical: 64-216 atoms")
    print()
    print("  Corrections needed:")
    print("    • Charged defect: Makov-Payne, FNV")
    print("    • Image interaction: Supercell extrapolation")
    print("    • Band filling: Occupation correction")

doping_defects()
```

<Tip>
For charged defect calculations, always apply finite-size corrections. The Freysoldt-Neugebauer-Van de Walle (FNV) correction is widely used and implemented in tools like pylada and CoFFEE.
</Tip>

## 6. High-Throughput Screening

```python
def high_throughput():
    """High-throughput screening for electronic materials."""

    print("High-Throughput Screening")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nWorkflow:")
    print("-" * 45)
    workflow = [
        "1. Define target properties (band gap, m*, stability)",
        "2. Generate candidate structures",
        "3. Fast screening (ML or simple DFT)",
        "4. Accurate calculation on survivors",
        "5. Validation and synthesis",
    ]
    for step in workflow:
        print(f"  {step}")

    print("\nDatabases:")
    print("-" * 45)
    databases = [
        ("Materials Project", "150k+", "mp-api.org"),
        ("AFLOW", "3M+", "aflowlib.org"),
        ("OQMD", "1M+", "oqmd.org"),
        ("C2DB", "15k+", "2D materials"),
    ]
    print(f"  {'Database':<20} {'Entries':<12} {'Notes'}")
    print("  " + "-" * 45)
    for db, entries, notes in databases:
        print(f"  {db:<20} {entries:<12} {notes}")

    print("\nDescriptors for Screening:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Electronic:")
    print("    • Band gap (in target range)")
    print("    • Effective mass (low for mobility)")
    print("    • Work function (for contacts)")
    print()
    print("  Stability:")
    print("    • Formation energy (< 0)")
    print("    • Energy above hull (< 50 meV/atom)")
    print("    • Phonon stability (no imaginary modes)")

    print("\nML Acceleration:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Train models on DFT data:")
    print("    • Band gap from structure")
    print("    • Formation energy")
    print("    • Predict before expensive calculation")

high_throughput()
```

<Warning>
High-throughput screening identifies candidates but doesn't guarantee synthesizability. Always verify thermodynamic stability and check for known synthesis routes before experimental effort.
</Warning>
