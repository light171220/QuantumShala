## 1. Regulatory Landscape

<Definition term="Computational Toxicology">
The use of computational methods to predict toxicological properties and support safety assessments, increasingly accepted by regulators as part of weight-of-evidence approaches.
</Definition>

## 6. Best Practices for Submissions

```python
def submission_practices():
    """Best practices for regulatory submissions."""

    print("Best Practices for Regulatory Submissions")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nDocumentation Standards:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Every computational study should include:")
    print()
    print("  1. Objective: What question was addressed")
    print("  2. Methods: Software, parameters, inputs")
    print("  3. Results: Outputs, with uncertainty")
    print("  4. Validation: Comparison to experiment")
    print("  5. Conclusion: Answer to objective")
    print("  6. Limitations: What we don't know")

    print("\nFigure/Table Presentations:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  • Clear labels and units")
    print("  • Error bars/confidence intervals")
    print("  • Comparison to experimental data")
    print("  • Applicability domain indication")

    print("\nCommon Pitfalls to Avoid:")
    print("-" * 45)
    pitfalls = [
        ("Over-claiming", "State limitations explicitly"),
        ("Black box", "Explain method rationale"),
        ("No validation", "Always compare to data"),
        ("Wrong domain", "Check applicability"),
        ("Poor documentation", "Keep audit trail"),
    ]
    for pitfall, solution in pitfalls:
        print(f"  {pitfall:<18}: {solution}")

    print("\nReviewer Questions to Anticipate:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  • How was the model validated?")
    print("  • Is the compound in applicability domain?")
    print("  • What is the uncertainty in prediction?")
    print("  • How does this compare to experimental data?")
    print("  • What are the consequences if wrong?")

    print("\nSuccess Factors:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ✓ Use computation to explain, not replace")
    print("  ✓ Always provide experimental validation")
    print("  ✓ Be transparent about limitations")
    print("  ✓ Use accepted/validated methods")
    print("  ✓ Document thoroughly")

submission_practices()
```

<Warning>
Never claim that computational predictions eliminate the need for experimental testing in regulatory submissions. Instead, present computation as supporting evidence that enhances scientific understanding and guides experimental design.
</Warning>
