## 1. The Mean-Field Idea

<Definition term="Mean-Field Approximation">
An approximation where each electron moves in the average (mean) electrostatic field created by all other electrons, rather than responding to their instantaneous positions. This replaces the many-body problem with many one-body problems.
</Definition>

```python
def mean_field_concept():
    """Illustrate the mean-field approximation."""

    print("The Mean-Field Approximation")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nThe Problem:")
    print("  Full Hamiltonian: Ĥ = Σᵢ ĥ(i) + Σᵢ<ⱼ 1/rᵢⱼ")
    print("  The 1/rᵢⱼ term couples ALL electrons together!")

    print("\nThe Approximation:")
    print("  Replace instantaneous e-e repulsion with average:")
    print("  Σᵢ<ⱼ 1/rᵢⱼ → Σᵢ V̄ᵢ(rᵢ)")
    print()
    print("  Each electron sees a one-body potential V̄")
    print("  that represents the AVERAGE field of all others")

    print("\nMathematically:")
    print("  True: ⟨Ψ|Σᵢ<ⱼ 1/rᵢⱼ|Ψ⟩")
    print("  Approx: Σᵢ ⟨φᵢ|V̄|φᵢ⟩ where V̄ = Ĵ - K̂")

    print("\nConsequence:")
    print("  • Many-body → many one-body problems")
    print("  • Wavefunction = single Slater determinant")
    print("  • Computationally tractable: O(N⁴)")
    print("  • Missing: electron correlation (instantaneous avoidance)")

mean_field_concept()
```

<Tip>
The mean-field approximation is like treating traffic flow: instead of tracking every car's position, we describe the average density. This works well for smooth traffic but misses "correlation" effects like cars bunching up.
</Tip>

## 2. The Variational Principle

Hartree-Fock finds the best single-determinant wavefunction:

<Formula>
$$E_{HF} = \min_{|\Psi_{SD}\rangle} \frac{\langle\Psi_{SD}|\hat{H}|\Psi_{SD}\rangle}{\langle\Psi_{SD}|\Psi_{SD}\rangle}$$
</Formula>

```python
def variational_principle():
    """Explain the variational approach to HF."""

    print("The Variational Principle in Hartree-Fock")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\n1. THE VARIATIONAL THEOREM:")
    print("   For any trial wavefunction |Ψ_trial⟩:")
    print()
    print("   E_trial = ⟨Ψ_trial|Ĥ|Ψ_trial⟩ ≥ E_exact")
    print()
    print("   Equality holds only when Ψ_trial = Ψ_exact")

    print("\n2. HARTREE-FOCK STRATEGY:")
    print("   Restrict search to Slater determinants:")
    print("   |Ψ_SD⟩ = |χ₁χ₂...χₙ⟩")
    print()
    print("   Find the BEST determinant:")
    print("   E_HF = min_{orbitals} E[Ψ_SD]")

    print("\n3. WHAT WE OPTIMIZE:")
    print("   The spin-orbitals χᵢ(x) = φᵢ(r)σᵢ(s)")
    print("   Subject to orthonormality: ⟨χᵢ|χⱼ⟩ = δᵢⱼ")

    print("\n4. RESULT:")
    print("   E_HF ≥ E_exact")
    print("   E_exact - E_HF = correlation energy (what we miss)")

variational_principle()
```

## 3. Energy Expression for a Slater Determinant

<Formula>
$$E_{HF} = \sum_i \langle\chi_i|\hat{h}|\chi_i\rangle + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{ij}\left[\langle\chi_i\chi_j|\chi_i\chi_j\rangle - \langle\chi_i\chi_j|\chi_j\chi_i\rangle\right]$$
</Formula>

```python
def hf_energy_expression():
    """Derive the HF energy expression."""

    print("Hartree-Fock Energy Expression")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nUsing Slater-Condon rules for ⟨Ψ_SD|Ĥ|Ψ_SD⟩:")

    print("\n1. ONE-ELECTRON PART:")
    print("   ⟨Ψ|Σᵢ ĥᵢ|Ψ⟩ = Σᵢ ⟨χᵢ|ĥ|χᵢ⟩")
    print()
    print("   where ĥ = -½∇² - Σₐ Zₐ/rₐ")
    print("   (kinetic energy + nuclear attraction)")

    print("\n2. TWO-ELECTRON PART:")
    print("   ⟨Ψ|Σᵢ<ⱼ 1/rᵢⱼ|Ψ⟩ = ½ Σᵢⱼ [Jᵢⱼ - Kᵢⱼ]")

    print("\n   COULOMB INTEGRAL (Jᵢⱼ):")
    print("   Jᵢⱼ = ∫∫ |χᵢ(1)|² (1/r₁₂) |χⱼ(2)|² dr₁dr₂")
    print("   Classical electron-electron repulsion")

    print("\n   EXCHANGE INTEGRAL (Kᵢⱼ):")
    print("   Kᵢⱼ = ∫∫ χᵢ*(1)χⱼ(1) (1/r₁₂) χⱼ*(2)χᵢ(2) dr₁dr₂")
    print("   Quantum mechanical - no classical analog!")
    print("   Arises from antisymmetry (fermion statistics)")

    print("\n3. TOTAL HF ENERGY:")
    print("   E_HF = Σᵢ hᵢᵢ + ½ Σᵢⱼ (Jᵢⱼ - Kᵢⱼ) + V_nn")
    print()
    print("   V_nn = nuclear-nuclear repulsion (constant)")

hf_energy_expression()
```

## 4. Coulomb and Exchange Operators

<Definition term="Coulomb Operator">
The operator Ĵⱼ that gives the average electrostatic repulsion from electron j: Ĵⱼ(1)φᵢ(1) = φᵢ(1)∫|χⱼ(2)|²/r₁₂ dr₂. This is a local, classical-like interaction.
</Definition>

<Definition term="Exchange Operator">
The operator K̂ⱼ that arises from antisymmetry: K̂ⱼ(1)φᵢ(1) = χⱼ(1)∫χⱼ*(2)φᵢ(2)/r₁₂ dr₂. It's non-local and has no classical analog—it "exchanges" the orbitals.
</Definition>

```python
def coulomb_exchange_operators():
    """Explain Coulomb and Exchange operators."""

    print("Coulomb and Exchange Operators")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\n1. COULOMB OPERATOR Ĵⱼ:")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("   Ĵⱼ(1)φᵢ(1) = [∫ |χⱼ(2)|²/r₁₂ dr₂] φᵢ(1)")
    print()
    print("   Properties:")
    print("   • LOCAL: Output at r depends only on input at r")
    print("   • Positive (repulsive)")
    print("   • Classical electrostatic interpretation")
    print("   • Exists for all electron pairs")

    print("\n2. EXCHANGE OPERATOR K̂ⱼ:")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("   K̂ⱼ(1)φᵢ(1) = [∫ χⱼ*(2)φᵢ(2)/r₁₂ dr₂] χⱼ(1)")
    print()
    print("   Properties:")
    print("   • NON-LOCAL: Output at r depends on input everywhere")
    print("   • Negative (stabilizing)")
    print("   • No classical interpretation - purely quantum!")
    print("   • Only for same-spin electrons")
    print("   • Reduces e-e repulsion between like spins")

    print("\n3. PHYSICAL MEANING OF EXCHANGE:")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("   • Creates 'Fermi hole' - same-spin electrons avoid each other")
    print("   • This is why parallel spins favor (Hund's rule)")
    print("   • Exchange stabilization ~ 1 eV per electron pair")

    print("\n4. TOTAL MEAN-FIELD POTENTIAL:")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("   V̂_HF = Σⱼ (Ĵⱼ - K̂ⱼ)")
    print("   This is the effective potential each electron feels")

coulomb_exchange_operators()
```

<Warning>
The exchange operator is non-local: to compute K̂ψ(r), you need to know ψ at ALL points, not just at r. This makes HF more expensive than pure Coulomb electrostatics.
</Warning>

## 5. The Hartree-Fock Equations

Minimizing the energy with respect to orbital variations gives:

<Formula>
$$\hat{f}|\chi_i\rangle = \varepsilon_i|\chi_i\rangle$$
</Formula>

where $\hat{f} = \hat{h} + \sum_j(\hat{J}_j - \hat{K}_j)$ is the Fock operator.

```python
def hartree_fock_equations():
    """Derive the Hartree-Fock equations."""

    print("The Hartree-Fock Equations")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nDERIVATION:")
    print("  Minimize E_HF subject to orthonormality ⟨χᵢ|χⱼ⟩ = δᵢⱼ")
    print("  Use Lagrange multipliers:")
    print()
    print("  L = E_HF - Σᵢⱼ εᵢⱼ(⟨χᵢ|χⱼ⟩ - δᵢⱼ)")
    print()
    print("  Setting δL/δχᵢ* = 0 gives:")

    print("\n  f̂|χᵢ⟩ = Σⱼ εᵢⱼ|χⱼ⟩")
    print()
    print("  Diagonalizing the εᵢⱼ matrix (canonical orbitals):")
    print()
    print("  f̂|χᵢ⟩ = εᵢ|χᵢ⟩  ← The Hartree-Fock Equations")

    print("\nTHE FOCK OPERATOR:")
    print("  f̂ = ĥ + Σⱼ(Ĵⱼ - K̂ⱼ)")
    print()
    print("  = -½∇² - Σₐ Zₐ/rₐ + V̂_HF")
    print()
    print("  One-electron operator with mean-field e-e repulsion")

    print("\nKEY INSIGHT:")
    print("  • Looks like an eigenvalue equation")
    print("  • BUT: f̂ depends on its own solutions!")
    print("  • Must solve SELF-CONSISTENTLY (iteratively)")

hartree_fock_equations()
```

## 6. Self-Consistency

The Fock operator depends on the orbitals we're trying to find:

```python
def self_consistency():
    """Explain the self-consistency requirement."""

    print("Self-Consistency in Hartree-Fock")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nTHE PROBLEM:")
    print("  f̂|χᵢ⟩ = εᵢ|χᵢ⟩")
    print()
    print("  BUT f̂ = ĥ + Σⱼ(Ĵⱼ - K̂ⱼ)")
    print()
    print("  The Ĵⱼ and K̂ⱼ operators depend on {χⱼ}!")
    print("  f̂ depends on its own solutions!")

    print("\nTHE SOLUTION: SELF-CONSISTENT FIELD (SCF)")
    print("-" * 50)
    print("  1. Guess initial orbitals {χ⁽⁰⁾}")
    print("  2. Build Fock operator f̂⁽⁰⁾")
    print("  3. Solve eigenvalue problem → {χ⁽¹⁾}")
    print("  4. Build new Fock operator f̂⁽¹⁾ from {χ⁽¹⁾}")
    print("  5. Repeat until {χ⁽ⁿ⁺¹⁾} ≈ {χ⁽ⁿ⁾}")

    print("\nCONVERGENCE CRITERIA:")
    print("  • Energy: |E⁽ⁿ⁺¹⁾ - E⁽ⁿ⁾| < threshold")
    print("  • Density: ||P⁽ⁿ⁺¹⁾ - P⁽ⁿ⁾|| < threshold")
    print("  • Orbital coefficients")
    print()
    print("  Typical: 10-20 iterations to converge")

self_consistency()
```

<Tip>
The term "Self-Consistent Field" (SCF) refers to this iterative procedure. A "converged" HF calculation means the orbitals used to build the Fock operator are the same as those that diagonalize it.
</Tip>

## 7. Physical Interpretation

```python
def physical_interpretation():
    """Explain what HF orbitals and energies mean."""

    print("Physical Interpretation of Hartree-Fock")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nORBITAL ENERGIES (εᵢ):")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("  Koopmans' theorem (approximate):")
    print("  • εᵢ ≈ -IP (ionization potential from orbital i)")
    print("  • εₐ ≈ -EA (electron affinity to orbital a)")
    print()
    print("  HOMO-LUMO gap ≈ excitation energy (crude)")

    print("\nTOTAL ENERGY:")
    print("-" * 40)
    print("  E_HF = Σᵢ εᵢ - ½Σᵢⱼ(Jᵢⱼ - Kᵢⱼ) + V_nn")
    print()
    print("  NOTE: E_HF ≠ Σᵢ εᵢ")
    print("  (Double counting of e-e interactions must be corrected)")

    print("\nWHAT HF GETS RIGHT:")
    print("-" * 40)
    right = [
        'Total energies (to ~1%)',
        'Equilibrium geometries (mostly)',
        'Harmonic frequencies (usually)',
        'Dipole moments (often)',
        'Qualitative bonding picture',
    ]
    for item in right:
        print(f"  ✓ {item}")

    print("\nWHAT HF GETS WRONG:")
    print("-" * 40)
    wrong = [
        'Bond dissociation (qualitatively wrong!)',
        'Reaction barriers (underestimated)',
        'Weak interactions (missing dispersion)',
        'Strongly correlated systems',
        'Absolute energies (need correlation)',
    ]
    for item in wrong:
        print(f"  ✗ {item}")

physical_interpretation()
```

## 8. Restricted vs Unrestricted HF

```python
def rhf_vs_uhf():
    """Compare RHF and UHF approaches."""

    print("Restricted vs Unrestricted Hartree-Fock")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\n1. RESTRICTED HARTREE-FOCK (RHF):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("   α and β electrons share spatial orbitals:")
    print("   χᵢᵅ(x) = φᵢ(r)α(s)")
    print("   χᵢᵝ(x) = φᵢ(r)β(s)")
    print()
    print("   • Same spatial orbital for paired electrons")
    print("   • N/2 spatial orbitals for N electrons")
    print("   • Only for closed-shell systems (S=0)")
    print("   • Wavefunction is spin eigenfunction")

    print("\n2. UNRESTRICTED HARTREE-FOCK (UHF):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("   α and β have different spatial orbitals:")
    print("   χᵢᵅ(x) = φᵢᵅ(r)α(s)")
    print("   χⱼᵝ(x) = φⱼᵝ(r)β(s)")
    print()
    print("   • Independent optimization of each spin")
    print("   • Lower energy than RHF")
    print("   • BUT: NOT a spin eigenfunction!")
    print("   • Suffers from 'spin contamination'")

    print("\n3. RESTRICTED OPEN-SHELL (ROHF):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("   Paired electrons share orbitals")
    print("   Unpaired electrons have unique orbitals")
    print()
    print("   • Proper spin eigenfunction")
    print("   • Higher energy than UHF")
    print("   • More complex Fock matrix")

rhf_vs_uhf()
```

## Summary

In this lesson, you learned:

1. **Mean-field approximation** replaces instantaneous e-e repulsion with average
2. **Variational principle** finds the optimal single determinant
3. **Coulomb operator** gives classical electrostatic repulsion
4. **Exchange operator** is a non-local, quantum effect
5. **Fock operator** defines the effective one-electron Hamiltonian
6. **Self-consistency** is required because f̂ depends on its solutions
7. **RHF vs UHF** trade-offs for open-shell systems

Hartree-Fock is the starting point for all of quantum chemistry.
