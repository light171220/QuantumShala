## 1. When Quantum Chemistry is Essential

<Definition term="QM in Drug Design">
The application of quantum mechanical methods to solve drug discovery problems that cannot be accurately addressed by classical force fields, including electronic effects, metal coordination, and covalent bonding.
</Definition>

## 2. QM-Based Binding Affinity

```python
def qm_binding():
    """Quantum mechanical binding energy calculations."""

    print("QM Binding Energy Calculations")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nEnergy Decomposition Analysis (EDA):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ΔE_bind = ΔE_elec + ΔE_pol + ΔE_disp + ΔE_exch + ΔE_ct")
    print()
    components = [
        ("Electrostatic", "ΔE_elec", "Classical Coulomb"),
        ("Polarization", "ΔE_pol", "Induced charges"),
        ("Dispersion", "ΔE_disp", "London forces"),
        ("Exchange", "ΔE_exch", "Pauli repulsion"),
        ("Charge transfer", "ΔE_ct", "Electron delocalization"),
    ]
    for name, symbol, description in components:
        print(f"  {name:<14} {symbol:<10} {description}")

    print("\nSAPT (Symmetry-Adapted Perturbation Theory):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Rigorous decomposition of interaction energy")
    print("  Insight into binding mechanism")
    print()
    print("  Example for H-bond:")
    print("    Electrostatic: -8.5 kcal/mol")
    print("    Exchange:      +6.2 kcal/mol")
    print("    Induction:     -2.1 kcal/mol")
    print("    Dispersion:    -1.8 kcal/mol")
    print("    Total:         -6.2 kcal/mol")

    print("\nFragment-Based Methods:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Problem: Full QM on protein too expensive")
    print()
    methods = [
        ("FMO", "Fragment Molecular Orbital", "Pair interactions"),
        ("MFCC", "Molecular Fractionation", "Conjugate caps"),
        ("EE-MBE", "Electrostatically Embedded", "Many-body"),
    ]
    for abbrev, name, approach in methods:
        print(f"  • {abbrev}: {name}")
        print(f"    Approach: {approach}")

qm_binding()
```

<Formula name="SAPT Interaction Energy">
$$E_{int}^{SAPT} = E_{elst}^{(1)} + E_{exch}^{(1)} + E_{ind}^{(2)} + E_{exch-ind}^{(2)} + E_{disp}^{(2)} + E_{exch-disp}^{(2)}$$

Providing a physically meaningful decomposition of the non-covalent interaction energy.
</Formula>

## 4. Covalent Inhibitor Design

```python
def covalent_inhibitors():
    """QM for covalent inhibitor design."""

    print("Covalent Inhibitor Design with QM")

    print("  Drug + Target-residue → Drug-Target (covalent)")

    print("  Warheads + nucleophilic residues:")

    print("\nQM Required for:")
    print("-" * 45)
    requirements = [
        ("Reaction barrier", "Kinact/KI ratio"),
        ("Selectivity", "Off-target reactivity"),
        ("Reversibility", "Adduct stability"),

    ]
    for what, why in requirements:
        print(f"  • {what}: {why}")

    print("\nMichael Addition Mechanism:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Cys-SH + CH₂=CH-C(=O)-R → Cys-S-CH₂-CH₂-C(=O)-R")
    print()
    print("  Key transition state:")
    print("    • S-C bond forming (~2.2 Å)")
    print("    • Proton transfer (base assistance)")
    print("    • Negative charge on enolate")
    print()
    print("  Barrier typically: 15-25 kcal/mol")

    print("\nQM/MM Protocol:")
    print("-" * 45)
    steps = [
        "1. Model binding pose (docking/MD)",
        "2. Define QM region (Cys + warhead + nearby)",
        "3. Optimize reactant complex",
        "4. Find transition state (QST2, NEB)",
        "5. Optimize product",
        "6. Calculate barrier and reaction energy",
    ]
    for step in steps:
        print(f"  {step}")

covalent_inhibitors()
```

<Tip>
For covalent inhibitor design, the reaction barrier determines kinact. A barrier too low leads to off-target reactivity; too high means slow inactivation. Target ΔG‡ ≈ 18-22 kcal/mol for selective covalent inhibitors.
</Tip>
