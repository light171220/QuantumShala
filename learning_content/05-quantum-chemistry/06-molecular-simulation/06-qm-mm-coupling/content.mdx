## 1. Introduction to QM/MM

<Definition term="QM/MM">
A multiscale simulation method that treats a small, chemically important region with quantum mechanics (QM) while describing the larger environment with molecular mechanics (MM).
</Definition>

```python
def qmmm_introduction():
    """Introduction to QM/MM methods."""

    print("Introduction to QM/MM Methods")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nThe Challenge:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  • Large systems: proteins, enzymes, materials")
    print("  • Need accuracy: reactions, charge transfer")
    print("  • Full QM: too expensive (>10⁵ atoms)")
    print("  • Pure MM: can't break bonds")

    print("\nThe Solution: QM/MM")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Divide system into regions:")
    print("    QM region: Active site, reaction center")
    print("    MM region: Environment, scaffold")
    print()
    print("  H_total = H_QM + H_MM + H_QM/MM")

    print("\nPartitioning:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  |←------ Environment (MM) -------|")
    print("  |   |←- Active (QM) -→|          |")
    print("  | MM | QM | QM | QM | MM | MM |  |")
    print()
    print("  QM: 10-100 atoms (expensive)")
    print("  MM: 1000-100000 atoms (cheap)")

    print("\nAdvantages:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  • Accurate QM where needed")
    print("  • Environmental effects included")
    print("  • Computationally feasible")
    print("  • 2013 Nobel Prize (Karplus, Levitt, Warshel)")

qmmm_introduction()
```

## 2. QM/MM Energy Expressions

<Formula>
$$E_{\text{QM/MM}} = E_{\text{QM}}(\mathbf{r}_{\text{QM}}) + E_{\text{MM}}(\mathbf{r}_{\text{MM}}) + E_{\text{QM/MM}}^{\text{int}}$$
</Formula>

```python
def qmmm_energy():
    """QM/MM energy expressions."""

    print("QM/MM Energy Components")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nTotal Energy:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  E = E_QM + E_MM + E_QM/MM")
    print()
    print("  E_QM: Quantum energy of QM region")
    print("  E_MM: Classical energy of MM region")
    print("  E_QM/MM: Interaction between regions")

    print("\nElectrostatic Coupling:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Electrostatic Embedding:")
    print("    MM charges polarize QM density")
    print("    V_ext = Σᵢ qᵢ/|r - Rᵢ|")
    print()
    print("  Mechanical Embedding:")
    print("    MM charges interact with QM Mulliken charges")
    print("    Simpler but less accurate")

    print("\nVan der Waals:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Usually classical (Lennard-Jones)")
    print("  E_vdW = Σᵢⱼ 4ε[(σ/r)¹² - (σ/r)⁶]")
    print()
    print("  QM atoms need LJ parameters too")

    print("\nPolarizable Embedding:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Advanced: MM region also responds")
    print("  Induced dipoles on MM atoms")
    print("  More accurate, more expensive")

qmmm_energy()
```

## 3. Boundary Treatments

```python
def boundary_treatments():
    """Handling QM/MM boundaries."""

    print("QM/MM Boundary Treatments")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nThe Boundary Problem:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  What if QM and MM regions share a bond?")
    print("  Can't just cut covalent bonds!")

    print("\nLink Atom Method:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Add 'capping' atom (usually H) at boundary")
    print()
    print("  Real:  QM-C-MM")
    print("  Model: QM-H (link atom)")
    print()
    print("  Link atom satisfies QM valence")
    print("  Position constrained to QM-MM axis")

    print("\nFrozen Orbital Methods:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Use localized orbitals at boundary")
    print("  'Freeze' some orbitals from MM atoms")
    print("  LSCF, GHO, IMOMM methods")

    print("\nQM/MM Boundary Best Practices:")
    print("-" * 45)
    practices = [
        "Cut only non-polar bonds (C-C, not C-N)",
        "Place boundary far from reaction",
        "Use large enough QM region",
        "Test convergence with QM region size",
    ]
    for p in practices:
        print(f"  • {p}")

boundary_treatments()
```

<Warning>
Place QM/MM boundaries carefully - cutting polar bonds or placing boundaries too close to the reaction can introduce significant errors. Test with larger QM regions when possible.
</Warning>

## 4. QM/MM Molecular Dynamics

```python
def qmmm_dynamics():
    """QM/MM molecular dynamics."""

    print("QM/MM Molecular Dynamics")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nQM/MM MD Algorithm:")
    print("-" * 45)
    steps = [
        "1. Partition system into QM and MM",
        "2. Compute QM energy and forces",
        "3. Include MM point charges in QM",
        "4. Compute MM forces (fast)",
        "5. Propagate all atoms with MD",
        "6. Repeat from step 2",
    ]
    for step in steps:
        print(f"  {step}")

    print("\nComputational Cost:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Dominated by QM calculation")
    print("  DFT on 50-100 atoms: 1-10 sec/step")
    print("  Total: ~10 ps - 1 ns feasible")

    print("\nQM Method Choices:")
    print("-" * 45)
    methods = [
        ("Semiempirical", "PM6, AM1", "Fast, ~100 atoms"),
        ("DFT", "B3LYP, PBE", "Accurate, 50-100 atoms"),
        ("ab initio", "MP2, CCSD", "Expensive, <30 atoms"),
    ]
    print(f"  {'Method':<15} {'Examples':<15} {'Use'}")
    print("  " + "-" * 45)
    for m, ex, use in methods:
        print(f"  {m:<15} {ex:<15} {use}")

    print("\nSoftware:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ChemShell, ONIOM (Gaussian), NAMD+TeraChem")
    print("  QM/MM interfaces in most QM packages")

qmmm_dynamics()
```
