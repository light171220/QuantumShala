## 1. Why Free Energy?

<Definition term="Free Energy">
The thermodynamic potential that determines equilibrium and spontaneity: G = H - TS (Gibbs) or A = U - TS (Helmholtz). Chemical processes favor lower free energy states.
</Definition>

```python

    """Why free energy calculations matter."""

    print("Importance of Free Energy")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nWhat Free Energy Tells Us:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ΔG < 0: Spontaneous process")
    print("  ΔG = 0: Equilibrium")
    print("  ΔG > 0: Non-spontaneous")

    print("\nRelation to Observables:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Equilibrium constant: K = exp(-ΔG/RT)")
    print("  Binding affinity: Kd = exp(ΔG_bind/RT)")

    print("  Reaction rates: ΔG‡ (activation)")

    print("\nApplications:")
    print("-" * 45)
    apps = [
        ("Drug design", "Binding affinities"),
        ("Materials", "Phase stability"),
        ("Catalysis", "Reaction barriers"),
        ("Biochemistry", "Protein folding"),
    ]
    for app, use in apps:
        print(f"  {app}: {use}")

    print("\nChallenges:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  • Cannot measure ΔG directly from MD")
```

## 2. Thermodynamic Integration

<Formula>
$$\Delta G = \int_0^1 \left\langle \frac{\partial H(\lambda)}{\partial \lambda} \right\rangle_\lambda d\lambda$$
</Formula>

```python
def thermodynamic_integration():
    """Thermodynamic integration method."""

    print("Thermodynamic Integration (TI)")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nConcept:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Connect initial (λ=0) and final (λ=1) states")
    print("  H(λ) = (1-λ)H₀ + λH₁")
    print("  Gradually transform one into the other")

    print("\nFree Energy Difference:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ΔG = ∫₀¹ ⟨∂H/∂λ⟩_λ dλ")
    print()
    print("  ⟨...⟩_λ = average at fixed λ")
    print("  Run simulations at multiple λ values")

    print("\nPractical TI:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  1. Choose λ points (e.g., 0, 0.1, 0.2, ..., 1)")
    print("  2. Run simulation at each λ")
    print("  3. Compute ⟨∂H/∂λ⟩ at each λ")
    print("  4. Integrate numerically")

    print("\nλ Scheduling:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Linear: Simple but inefficient")
    print("  Gaussian: More points where ⟨∂H/∂λ⟩ varies")
    print("  Soft-core: Avoid singularities")

    print("\nExample: Solvation Free Energy")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  λ=0: Solute in vacuum")
    print("  λ=1: Solute in solvent")
    print("  ΔG = G(solvent) - G(vacuum)")
```

## 3. Free Energy Perturbation

```python
def free_energy_perturbation():
    """Free energy perturbation method."""

    print("Free Energy Perturbation (FEP)")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nZwanzig Equation:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  ΔG = -kT ln⟨exp(-(H₁-H₀)/kT)⟩₀")
    print()
    print("  Sample from state 0, evaluate energy diff")
    print("  Exponential average gives ΔG")

    print("\nForward and Backward:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Forward:  ΔG₀→₁ = -kT ln⟨exp(-ΔH/kT)⟩₀")
    print("  Backward: ΔG₁→₀ = -kT ln⟨exp(+ΔH/kT)⟩₁")
    print()
    print("  Should give opposite results if converged")
    print("  BAR: Bennett Acceptance Ratio (optimal)")

    print("\nStaged FEP:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Large ΔH: poor overlap, bad convergence")
    print("  Solution: Many small steps")
    print("  ΔG = Σᵢ ΔGᵢ→ᵢ₊₁")

    print("\nBAR Method:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Uses both forward and backward sampling")
    print("  Minimizes variance")
    print("  Solve self-consistent equation for ΔG")

free_energy_perturbation()
```

<Tip>
FEP works best when states are similar. For large changes, use staging (multiple intermediate λ values) to ensure good overlap between adjacent states.
</Tip>

## 4. Umbrella Sampling

```python
def umbrella_sampling():
    """Umbrella sampling for free energy profiles."""

    print("Umbrella Sampling")
    print("=" * 60)

    print("\nProblem:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Want free energy along reaction coordinate ξ")
    print("  G(ξ) = -kT ln P(ξ) + const")
    print("  But: rare events not sampled naturally")

    print("\nSolution:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Add biasing potential (umbrella)")
    print("  W(ξ) = ½k(ξ - ξ₀)²")
    print()
    print("  Forces sampling around ξ₀")
    print("  Use different ξ₀ values (windows)")

    print("\nWHAM (Weighted Histogram Analysis):")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  Combine data from all windows")
    print("  Unbias and construct full G(ξ)")
    print("  Iterative self-consistent procedure")

    print("\nProcedure:")
    print("-" * 45)
    steps = [
        "1. Define reaction coordinate ξ",
        "2. Choose window centers ξ₀ᵢ",
        "3. Run simulation at each window",
        "4. Collect histograms P(ξ|ξ₀ᵢ)",
        "5. Apply WHAM to combine",
        "6. Obtain G(ξ) (PMF)",
    ]
    for step in steps:
        print(f"  {step}")

    print("\nPMF = Potential of Mean Force:")
    print("-" * 45)
    print("  G(ξ) along reaction coordinate")
    print("  Barrier height = activation energy")
    print("  Well depth = binding strength")

umbrella_sampling()
```
