## 1. What Is Quantum Advantage?

Quantum advantage occurs when a quantum computer solves a problem faster, more accurately, or more efficiently than any classical computer.

<Definition term="Quantum Advantage">
Quantum advantage is achieved when a quantum algorithm outperforms the best known classical algorithm for a practical problem, considering all resources (time, memory, energy).
</Definition>

### Types of Quantum Speedup

| Speedup Type | Classical | Quantum | Example |
|--------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Polynomial | $O(N)$ | $O(\sqrt{N})$ | Grover's search |
| Polynomial | $O(N^2)$ | $O(N)$ | Some linear algebra |
| Exponential | $O(2^N)$ | $O(N^k)$ | Integer factoring |

<Warning>
Not all problems benefit from quantum computing. The advantage depends on the specific problem structure and the best classical algorithms available.
</Warning>

## 3. Quantum Algorithms for Finance

### Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA)

QAOA is designed for combinatorial optimization problems.

<Definition term="QAOA">
QAOA is a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm that uses a parameterized quantum circuit to find approximate solutions to optimization problems, with parameters tuned classically.
</Definition>

**Financial Applications:**
- Portfolio optimization with discrete constraints
- Asset selection (choosing k assets from n)
- Trading strategy optimization


### Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE)

VQE finds ground states of Hamiltonians, applicable to optimization problems.

<Definition term="VQE">
VQE is a hybrid algorithm that prepares parameterized quantum states and variationally minimizes the expectation value of a problem Hamiltonian.
</Definition>

**Financial Applications:**
- Continuous portfolio optimization
- Risk minimization
- Factor model estimation

### Quantum Amplitude Estimation (QAE)

QAE provides quadratic speedup for Monte Carlo estimation.

<Definition term="Quantum Amplitude Estimation">
QAE estimates the probability of a quantum measurement outcome with $O(1/\epsilon)$ queries instead of the classical $O(1/\epsilon^2)$.
</Definition>

```python
# Classical vs Quantum Monte Carlo comparison
def monte_carlo_comparison(target_error=0.01):
    """
    Compare classical and quantum Monte Carlo requirements.
    """
    # Classical: error ~ 1/sqrt(N)
    classical_samples = int(1 / target_error**2)

    # Quantum: error ~ 1/N (with amplitude estimation)
    quantum_queries = int(1 / target_error)

    speedup = classical_samples / quantum_queries

    print(f"Target error: {target_error:.2%}")
    print(f"Classical samples needed: {classical_samples:,}")
    print(f"Quantum queries needed: {quantum_queries:,}")
    print(f"Quantum speedup: {speedup:.0f}x")

monte_carlo_comparison(0.001)  # 0.1% accuracy
```

**Financial Applications:**
- Option pricing
- Value at Risk (VaR)
- Expected shortfall
- Credit valuation adjustment (CVA)

## 4. Current State: NISQ Era

We are in the Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) era with significant limitations.

<Definition term="NISQ">
NISQ refers to current quantum computers with 50-1000+ qubits that are noisy (error-prone) and lack full error correction.
</Definition>

### Current Limitations

| Limitation | Impact | Mitigation |
|------------|--------|------------|
| **Noise** | Errors accumulate, limiting circuit depth | Error mitigation techniques |
| **Qubit count** | Limited problem sizes | Hybrid quantum-classical methods |
| **Connectivity** | Not all qubits connected | SWAP gate overhead |
| **Gate fidelity** | ~99.5% per gate | Shorter circuits, error correction |

```python
# Estimating circuit success probability
def circuit_success_probability(n_gates, gate_fidelity=0.995):
    """
    Estimate probability that entire circuit executes correctly.
    """
    success_prob = gate_fidelity ** n_gates

    print(f"Number of gates: {n_gates}")
    print(f"Gate fidelity: {gate_fidelity:.3%}")
    print(f"Circuit success probability: {success_prob:.4%}")

    return success_prob

# Typical circuits for financial problems
circuit_success_probability(100)   # Shallow circuit
circuit_success_probability(1000)  # Medium circuit
circuit_success_probability(10000) # Deep circuit - very low success
```

### Hybrid Quantum-Classical Approaches

NISQ algorithms use classical computers to handle optimization and error processing:

```
│  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │ Quantum Computer                         │   │

```

<Tip>
Near-term quantum advantage in finance will likely come from hybrid algorithms that leverage quantum computers for the hardest computational kernels while using classical computers for everything else.
</Tip>
