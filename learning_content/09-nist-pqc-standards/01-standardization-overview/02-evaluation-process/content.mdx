## Summary

NIST's evaluation methodology for post-quantum cryptography was comprehensive and transparent:

1. **Multi-Round Structure**: Three main rounds over 8 years allowed thorough analysis
2. **Security First**: Primary criterion, with five security categories defined
3. **Performance Matters**: Speed and size evaluated for practical deployment
4. **Public Cryptanalysis**: Global research community improved and tested candidates
5. **Diversity Preserved**: Selected schemes from different mathematical families
6. **Transparent Decisions**: Detailed rationale published for all choices

## Key Takeaways

- Security is paramount but must be balanced with practical usability
- Open evaluation processes catch flaws that closed processes miss
- Mathematical diversity provides insurance against unexpected breaks
- The SIKE and Rainbow breaks validate the importance of thorough analysis
- NIST's methodology serves as a model for future standardization efforts
